## 2. The custom-order flow ("Request a machine")

The intake that turns a stranger into a scoped build. Keep it to 5 fields — founder-language friendly:

1. **What's the job?** (one sentence — the outcome you want)
2. **Inputs** — what the machine gets to work with.
3. **Output** — what it must produce.
4. **Never-rules** — what it must never do (the guardrails / what can't break).
5. **Where it runs / who pays** — your stack or ours; timeline.

→ You convert this into a **Blueprint + Execution Contract** (your kernel already speaks this), scope a price, and build. The deliverable is a gated machine **+ its receipts**. This *is* productized consulting that compounds into reusable blueprints.

## 3. The Proof Sandbox — hook design (the smart, balanced part)

**Why a free proof beats a free trial:** a trial exposes your machine; a *proof* exposes only the result. The prospect experiences your one true differentiator — verifiable execution — while your internals stay sealed.

**Mechanics:**
- They submit **one bounded job** (via the 5-field intake).
- You run it through the real factory, gated, **once**.
- They receive: the **output** + a **shareable receipt** (artifact · evidence · decision) — the same object that's your audit trail.
- The receipt itself carries the pitch: *"this is what every job looks like."*

**Why it protects you (the balance you asked for):**
- They see the **what** (output) and the **proof** (it passed), never the **how** (blueprint, routing, contract, kernel).
- It's a **qualified lead magnet** — only people with a real job apply, and the job tells you their use case.
- Each free run is itself **gated and logged** — so the free tier can't become an abuse or data-exfiltration vector. Cap scope, size, and one-per-prospect.

**Limits to set (founder decision):** 1 run per prospect · max input size · no sensitive/regulated data in the free tier · sandbox runs flagged + rate-limited.

**The upsell is automatic:** "Loved your proof? The next runs are a paid seat, or we build you a dedicated machine." The receipt did the selling.
