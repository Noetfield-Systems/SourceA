## Executive summary

Canada’s **AI for All** strategy (~**$2.3B+** new federal commitment, June 4 2026) centers on **Trust · Opportunity · Sovereignty**. For your stack, the **highest-fit public lane** is **TrustField** (RPAA governance, audit, Canadian fintech trust). **Noetfield** is strong **on paper** but **pre-product**. **SourceA** is internal infrastructure — not a grant SKU. **Do not** reposition DevBridge/Cursor OS Pro as compliance products for this wave.

**Unblocking actions (90 days):** register procurement readiness, draft 2 funding briefs (TrustField + Noetfield spec), book TrustField demos, watch B.C. AI Action Plan.

## Strategic lanes (pick 2 max for 90 days)

### Lane A — TrustField GTM + procurement (PRIMARY)

**Thesis:** Sell **RPAA readiness + responsible AI governance** to Canadian FIs and payment actors — matches Trust + sovereignty without breaking engineering freeze.

**Actions:** CanadaBuys registration, 1-pager, 3 demo calls, PacifiCan/LIFT inquiry, SMBPP NAICS codes.

### Lane B — Noetfield positioning doc (SECONDARY, docs only)

**Thesis:** Frame future product as **trustworthy institutional AI / compliance** — do not build until SSOT activates.

**Actions:** Spec chapter aligning to AI Safety Institute / certification discourse; no Python scaffold.

### Lane C — Sovereign compute / data centre (OPTIONAL, only if ASF wants infra play)

**Thesis:** BC Hydro 400MW process — **only** if pursuing hosting/compute partnership, not default for SaaS founders.
