## One sentence

> **Noetfield** gives your board cryptographic proof of what Microsoft Copilot was **permitted** to do, under which **policy version** — with governance receipts on **every in-scope action**, not a quarterly assessment.

---

**The problem.** Your Copilot program is live — or your agents are in production. Most teams still govern at session start, if at all. When something goes wrong there is no signed record of what the agent was allowed to do, what it did, or whether the log was tampered with. Boards and regulators now ask: *prove what every material action was permitted to do, and prove the record is intact.*

**What Noetfield does.** Noetfield governs Copilot and agent execution **before the model acts**. Every in-scope action is gated by policy at dispatch, written to a signed, replayable receipt ledger, and protected by tamper detection.

**The proof — in under five minutes.** Not a slide. A live chain:

> **request → policy evaluation → decision → enforcement → signed receipt → replay → tamper-FAIL → signed audit chain**

You will see an action **blocked**, an action **allowed**, a tampered record **fail verification**, and the sequence **replayed** from the signed ledger — cold start, in five minutes.
