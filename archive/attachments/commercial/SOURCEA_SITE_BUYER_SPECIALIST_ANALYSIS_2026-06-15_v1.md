## Big picture — what story the site tells

The site runs **three narratives at once**:

1. **Infrastructure buyer** → policy · receipt · replay  
2. **Agency / revenue buyer** → growth flywheel · pipeline · MRR  
3. **Technical evaluator** → scenario theater · proof chain · loops  

All three converge on **Book live proof**.

That’s **strategically correct** for SourceA’s dual lane (platform eng + DFY agencies), but it creates **cognitive load on home**: hero sells proof infra *and* agentic revenue team *and* live pipeline console in one fold.

A disciplined buyer asks: *“Are you selling governance software, or a services firm that runs my GTM?”*  
The site answers: **both** — which is differentiated, but needs one sentence to unify (you’re close with “full proof row”).

## What would make me lean in (strengths)

1. **Proof is the product, not a feature**  
   Proof chain, terminal replay, BLOCK + tamper-FAIL, “&lt;5 min live demo” — that matches what boards ask in 2026 (*prove what ran, prove it wasn’t altered*).

2. **Compare matrix is honest and sharp**  
   You don’t pretend to be Temporal/LangGraph — you position as the layer above. Smart for technical buyers who already have orchestration.

3. **Fixed pricing without hourly surprise**  
   $750 audit → $3–10K build → retainer is a **procurement-friendly ladder**. Deposit-on-scope, balance-on-PASS is credible.

4. **Motion and console polish**  
   Orbit, pipeline feed, scenario council — reads **Series A product marketing**, not a freelancer site. First sight competes with [external-design-benchmark]/Notenic visually.

5. **Eval path**  
   `sourcea-boot` on GitHub signals “try before sales call” — infra buyers respect that.

6. **Category ambition**  
   “Reference standard” + naming competitors on Compare page — confident positioning; I’d take the call just to stress-test the claim.

## What would make me pause (risks)

1. **Trust gap vs [external-design-benchmark]/Credo tier**  
   Quote is anonymous (“Head of Platform Engineering, Series B SaaS”). No logos, no Forrester/Gartner strip, no “used by X teams.” At a high-grade company, **social proof is the missing row** in your own matrix.

2. **Two-buyer tension on home**  
   “Platform teams and revenue agencies” + $24K pipeline + “Brain/Outreach/Prove” — a CISO-minded buyer wonders if this is GTM automation dressed as governance. **Platform page + proof chain** recover trust; home should route faster.

3. **Named competitors on Compare**  
   Bold for outbound, risky for legal/procurement packets unless substantiated. Enterprise buyers may ask for **third-party validation** of the matrix.

4. **Metrics without source**  
   34% proof-to-close, 11d discovery→deposit, $24K pipeline — compelling for agencies, but a skeptical buyer labels them **marketing numbers** unless footnoted “illustrative” or “from design partners.”

5. **Home length**  
   Reference band → quote → team → platform → growth → sandbox → compare → pricing → explore — **excellent for eval**, heavy for **email attach**. One-pager still wins first outbound touch.

6. **Enterprise procurement checklist gaps** (not on site — buyer will ask anyway)  
   SOC2, data residency, SIEM export, MSA/SOW templates, security whitepaper — disk SSOT honestly says GTM is weaker here than [external-design-benchmark]/Credo.
