## Executive summary

The Lower Mainland pool/spa market has **~35–45 true dedicated service brands** but **300+ directory listings** (HomeStars, BBB, Yellow Pages) that inflate search . Fewer than **10 operators** combine online booking, recurring maintenance, and post-visit digital proof.

**Winning model (synthesized from Tier 1):**

> Book online → fixed route day/time → visit proof (photo + chemistry) → monthly plan, no contract → same tech every week → upsell open/close + repairs when equipment fails.

**Pure Flow positioning:**

> *Vancouver's book-first pool & spa service — same tech, same day, proof after every visit.*

**Zone 1 launch:** Kitsilano → Kerrisdale → Point Grey → West Side → North Shore wedge. Density beats "all Lower Mainland."

---

## 1. Market reality (Vancouver ≠ Sun Belt)

| Factor | Implication for Pure Flow |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Short swim season (~May–Sept serious use) | Opening/closing + weekly care are high-intent bookings |
| Soft water / rain dilution | Chemistry expertise is a trust signal (Capilano water, salt cells) |
| High home values, time-poor owners | West Side / North Shore pay for **reliability + proof**, not lowest price |
| Strata/commercial is separate | Lions Gate, Imperial, Bright, Pacific own health-authority compliance — avoid at launch |
| Hot tubs > pools in density | Spa weekly plans ($89–$159/mo) are a real entry product |
| Fragmented "pool guy" problem | Reviews cite no-shows, no reports, chaotic invoicing — **major whitespace** |

## 3. Website pattern library (what winners do)

| Pattern | Who does it well | Pure Flow status |
|---------|------------------|------------------|
| "Book online" primary CTA | Puddle, Crystalview | Done — add real scheduler next |
| Published starting prices | Lions Gate (spa), Splash | Packages exist — add **$ anchors** |
| Post-visit digital report | Puddle E-Report / Happy Snappy | **Missing** — biggest trust differentiator |
| Hyperlocal SEO pages | Puddle per neighborhood | Single page — add Kits / Kerrisdale / West Van |
| "We answer the phone" | Puddle | Placeholder phone — **critical fix** |
| No long-term contract | Puddle | Add explicitly to perks |
| Same technician, same window | West Coast, Mint | Add to how-it-works |
| Seasonal open/close booking | Puddle, Lions Gate | On site — push in spring/fall hero |
| Review social proof | All Tier 1 | Real Google reviews when live |
| Retail + service | Imperial, Crystalview, C-Blu | Phase 2 |

**Tone lanes:**

- **Puddle / Mint / Crystalview** → friendly homeowner, book a visit
- **Pacific Pools / Imperial commercial** → technical, compliance, strata
- **Pond's** → repair/renovation, "free assessment" (heavier)

**Pure Flow stays in Puddle/Mint lane** — service to people, not audit.

## 8. Strategic takeaway

The market is **not crowded with good bookable service** — it is crowded with **names**. Pure Flow's realistic win: own **Zone 1 residential booking UX** — friendlier than Pacific Pools, more focused than Puddle, more scalable than Mint — with **visit proof** and **no-contract** as the two promises bad reviews prove customers want.
