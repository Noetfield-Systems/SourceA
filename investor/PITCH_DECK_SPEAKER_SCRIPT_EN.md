# Speaker script — pitch deck (English)

**Use:** Read aloud or memorize. ~12–15 minutes with demo.  
**Pace:** Calm, business-first. Skip appendix unless asked.

---

## Slide 1 — Title (15 sec)

“Good [morning/afternoon]. I’m ASF. This is **Sina portfolio OS**.

One founder, several real businesses, remote operations, and **results customers pay for** — not lab tests.

Today I’ll show you how we run the portfolio, what’s already working, and what we’re shipping in the next ninety days.”

---

## Slide 2 — The human problem (45 sec)

“Here’s the problem I lived.

I don’t run one startup. I run **several operating companies** — platforms, commerce, a foundation, a future enterprise product.

Classically, when I leave my Mac, work **stops**. Five repos, five chats, no single picture of what actually shipped today.

Investors never funded ‘I can SSH from my phone.’ They fund **live products, pilots, and revenue** — per company.

So we built for **that** outcome, not for demos.”

---

## Slide 3 — What we built (30 sec)

“Layer zero — already done — is **how we operate**.

The Mac is the production floor: code, AI-assisted build, deploy scripts.

The phone is **remote start and status** — at home on Wi‑Fi, or away on Tailscale — same pipeline.

One spine ranks **which company is on deck today** and writes **today’s work orders**.

**Important:** I am **not** selling you this control layer as the product. It’s our **operating advantage**. What we sell is what each company ships.”

---

## Slide 4 — What we sell (60 sec)

“Here’s what **customers** pay for.

**TrustField** — trust and field operations platform for B2B clients.

**VIRLUX** — commerce and luxury stack — staging toward paying pilots.

**Sinaai Mono** — runtime spine and control plane — approvals, Telegram ops, partner integrations.

**777 Foundation** — public mission, site, partnerships, field programs.

**Noetfield** — enterprise knowledge and audit — spec today, build next.

**Separate program** — Cursor OS Pro — App Store remote IDE. **Different deck**, different revenue line. I won’t mix it into this round unless you want that SKU only.”

---

## Slide 5 — Demo frame (30 sec + demo)

“If we demo, here’s the frame.

Phone on **cellular** — Wi‑Fi can be off — open our remote desk, start a **production** run — not a five-second test.

The Mac runs the real pipeline; you see the log on the phone.

Then we **pivot immediately** to what matters: **VIRLUX staging**, **TrustField demo**, a URL, a pilot LOI — whatever is live **this week**.

[DO DEMO 2 min max — then pivot to business artifact.]”

---

## Slide 6 — Architecture (45 sec)

“Simple picture.

Phone connects to a **remote desk** on the Mac. Mac agent talks to **SinaPromptOS** — the brain: dispatch, ingest, architect refresh.

From there, work flows into **each company’s repo** — TrustField, VIRLUX, Mono, 777, Noetfield — and into **Source A**, our governance law: priority, ports, locked plans.

One truth for humans and for AI — so we don’t drift across five random folders.”

---

## Slide 7 — Traction (60 sec)

“Honest traction — June twenty twenty-six.

**Done:** Remote production from the phone — home and Tailscale. Multi-repo dispatch and ingest. Locked lanes so portfolio and App Store don’t collide. Ranked ecosystem priority.

**In progress:** Safe auto-handoff of work orders into every Cursor chat — today manual or one company at a time from the phone. And **customer-visible** milestones: staging, pilots, TestFlight for the separate app.

**Not claiming** aggregate ARR on this slide — we fill that when contracts are on the table. I won’t fake a revenue number in this meeting.”

---

## Slide 8 — Why now (40 sec)

“Why now?

Multi-company founders exist; **integrated ops** for them almost doesn’t.

AI coding tools are everywhere; **orchestration and governance** are the gap.

Remote work is permanent. **Starting production from the phone** is a real edge — but only if it shortens time to **live URL and paying pilot**.”

---

## Slide 9 — Moat (45 sec)

“Moat is four things.

One — **governance stack**, not five chaotic repos.

Two — **Prompt OS brain** — dispatch, ingest, execution logs, architect cycle.

Three — **remote ops proven** — same pipeline away from home.

Four — **separation** — internal orchestra vs commercial App Store SKU. We don’t confuse buyers or investors.”

---

## Slide 10 — Business model (45 sec)

“Revenue is **per entity**.

TrustField — B2B SaaS and services. VIRLUX — commerce and B2B pilots. Mono — platform and partners. 777 — grants and programs. Noetfield — enterprise license after build. Cursor OS Pro — consumer subscription — **separate story**.

HoldCo value is **speed, oversight, and less founder bottleneck** — not selling ‘automation PASS’ as a product.”

---

## Slide 11 — 90-day roadmap (40 sec)

“Next ninety days: **one customer-visible deliverable per company**.

Lead metric: **live URLs, pilots, paying accounts** — not how many times we pressed RUN on the phone.

VIRLUX — staging to pilot. TrustField — production demo. Mono — spine and approval gate. 777 — site and field execution. Noetfield — spec to MVP scope.

Details are in the roadmap PDF I can leave with you.”

---

## Slide 12 — Use of funds (40 sec)

“If we raise, capital maps to **named outcomes** — template on screen.

Roughly: forty percent VIRLUX and TrustField to **revenue** — pilots to contracts.

Twenty percent Mono spine and ops. Fifteen percent Noetfield build activation. Ten percent 777 brand and field. Fifteen percent ops safety — remove the paste bottleneck.

We adjust percentages per round; we **do not** fund more connectivity tests.”

---

## Slide 13 — Team (25 sec)

“Team today: **ASF** — founder, portfolio CEO, final authority on product and registry.

Execution is **AI-assisted with human approval** — I am the override.

We need advisors: regtech and commerce GTM, App Store growth for Pro, enterprise sales for Noetfield.”

---

## Slide 14 — Risks (35 sec)

“Risks and mitigations.

Founder bottleneck — remote ops and ranked dispatch.

AI reliability — VERIFY gates, human ingest, no blind auto-merge.

Scope creep — locked lanes; one company’s blocker doesn’t stop another’s ship.

Security — local-first Mac, Tailscale, no secrets in git.”

---

## Slide 15 — Ask (30 sec)

“My ask today:

**Introductions** to a first paying pilot — VIRLUX or TrustField.

An **advisor** who has run a multi-company portfolio.

If capital fits, funding tied to **named milestones** in the roadmap — not R&D on phone UI.”

---

## Slide 16 — Close (20 sec)

“Close.

We are **not** a university automation lab.

We are a **multi-business operator** with a remote production floor.

The next meeting should be: **live demo of what a customer pays for** — URL, pilot, contract.

Technical proof logs are appendix only. Thank you.”
